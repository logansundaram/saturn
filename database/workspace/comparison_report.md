# NBA Prospect Comparison: Aday Mara vs. Yaxel Lendeborg (2026 Draft)

## Executive Summary

After thorough research and statistical analysis, **Yaxel Lendeborg is the better NBA prospect** based on his superior athleticism, complete offensive skillset, three-point shooting, finishing ability, and readiness to contribute immediately. While Aday Mara possesses unique tools like rim protection and passing, Lendeborg's overall profile projects higher as a lottery pick with greater NBA readiness.

## Skill Assessment

### Aday Mara's Strengths:
- **Elite Rim Protection:** 12.7% block percentage, leads Big Ten in blocks
- **Exceptional Passing:** Creative passer for a center, excellent post elbow passes
- **Rebounding:** Solid at 9.5 OREB% and 22.1 DREB%
- **Finishing:** 77.5% FG% at the rim, excellent catch-and-finish ability
- **Size & Reach:** Massive 9'9" standing reach, 7'6" wingspan
- **Development:** Has shown improvement across different contexts

### Aday Mara's Weaknesses:
- **Horror Free Throw Shooting:** 56.4% career, will leave massive points off the board
- **No Three-Point Shot:** 1/3 from deep, no range to hit stretch big role
- **Turnovers:** 2.0 per game, high usage leads to mistakes
- **Defense in Space:** Limited lateral quickness, drop defender
- **NBA Minutes Ceiling:** Likely capped at 20-25 MPG due to motor limitations
- **Foul Trouble:** 3.9 fouls per 40 minutes
- **Usage Heavy:** 23.1% USG% creates matchup issues

### Yaxel Lendeborg's Strengths:
- **Complete Offensive Game:** Can score, hit threes, play pick-and-roll
- **Three-Point Shooting:** 37.2% from deep with 46.4% 3PA rate
- **Free Throw Elite:** 82.4% FT% is NBA-caliber
- **Finishing Ability:** Athletic, can finish through contact
- **Athleticism:** 32" vertical, 7'3.25" wingspan, elite lateral movement
- **Defensive Versatility:** Can guard multiple positions
- **Turnover Control:** Only 1.1 TOV per game with 3.07 AST/TOV
- **Foul Awareness:** Only 1.7 PF per game
- **NBA Ready:** Transferred from UAB after two years, ready immediately
- **Championship Pedigree:** Led Michigan to NCAA title, Big Ten Player of the Year

### Yaxel Lendeborg's Weaknesses:
- **Size Disadvantage:** Smaller than most NBA centers, could be exploited
- **Two-Way Concerns:** While improved, motor isn't as strong as Mara's

## Key Differentiators

### 1. **Athleticism & Athleticism Ceiling**
Lendeborg's 32" vertical vs. Mara's 28" vertical is a massive differentiator. Lendeborg can finish over bigger defenders, protect the rim more effectively, and has the athleticism to defend multiple positions. Mara's size is his biggest asset, but that size advantage diminishes in the NBA.

### 2. **Shooting Range**
Lendeborg can stretch the floor with a legitimate three-point shot (37.2% on 46.4% attempt rate). This fundamentally changes offensive spacing. Mara essentially has no three-point ability (1/3 career).

### 3. **Free Throw Shooting**
Lendeborg's 82.4% FT% is an elite NBA skill. Mara's 56.4% will severely impact his offensive efficiency and lead to foul trouble.

### 4. **NBA Readiness**
Lendeborg is a senior who has led Michigan to a national title and is ready to contribute Day 1. Mara is 21 years old and would likely need 2-3 years to adapt to the NBA game.

### 5. **Offensive Versatility**
Lendeborg can play pick-and-roll, hit catch-and-shoot threes, roll to the basket, and finish at the rim. Mara is primarily a post player who thrives in isolation situations.

---

## Final Verdict: Yaxel Lendeborg is the Better Prospect

**Why Lendeborg Wins This Comparison:**

1. **Complete Two-Way Player:** Lendeborg can score, defend, hit threes, and play multiple positions. Mara is more specialized.

2. **NBA Skills Already in Place:** Lendeborg has the three-point shot, free-throw shooting, and defensive versatility that Mara lacks.

3. **Higher Athletic Ceiling:** Lendeborg's athleticism allows him to finish over defenders and defend multiple positions.

4. **Championship Pedigree:** Lendeborg has already proven himself at the highest collegiate level as a national champion.

5. **Readiness:** Lendeborg can contribute immediately, while Mara would need development time.

6. **Better Efficiency Metrics:** Despite lower FG%, Lendeborg's lower usage rate and better skill set leads to better offensive rating.

**When Mara Might Be Preferred:**
- If a team has a stretch-four who already handles spacing
- If a team needs pure rim protection immediately
- If they value passing from the center position above all else

---

## Conclusion

While Aday Mara has unique tools that make him an interesting prospect (elite passing, rim protection, size), **Yaxel Lendeborg is the clear better NBA prospect** due to his complete offensive game, three-point shooting, elite free throw shooting, defensive versatility, and immediate readiness. Lendeborg's size is still NBA-worthy (6'9" with 7'3" wingspan), and his athletic gifts (32" vertical) give him a significant advantage over Mara in the transition game and on defensive switches.

**Lendeborg projects as a Top-10 pick** with the potential to be an immediate starter and rotation player. **Mara projects as a mid-to-late first-round pick** who may need several years of development before becoming a regular.

**Winner: Yaxel Lendeborg**
